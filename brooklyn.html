Gentrification in Brooklyn

This is a image of Kobe Bryant and his daugther, Vanessa who tragedically died. This mural is an image of their fans morning them. Right above this mural is redevolpment occuring which signifies the gentrification happening in this neighborhoods.
During the 1990s to 2000s using data collection from the Census, researchers found that middle class black families gentrifies low class black families because the neighborhood increased in value and there is racial diversity in the neighborhoods. This means that the family will experience less microagressions and racism. There was a limitation to the study that the Census couldn't exactly be precise in identifying gentrification. This brings to question how can gentification be identified accurately.
From 2000 to 2010, an increase of 6,700 to 15,6000 white people have moved into neighborhoods that were predonimantly Black and Latino. This lead to the decrease of Black and Latino residents. This is displacement which is when homes or apartments are being left by residents that can not afford the high prices. Brooklyn natives are leaving due to the big increase in price, facing harassments from tenants and witholding repairs for they can get tenats that pay higher. Black Brooklyn is known as areas in Brooklyn that have majority of the African American population. These areas would include Fort Greene, Clintin Hill Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brownsville, Crown Heights, Ocean Hill, Canarsie,Flatlands, East Flatbush, Flatbush, some parts of Bushwick, and some parts of downtown Brooklyn.
The development of the Barclays Center has increased amount of people living in the neighborhood and increased businesses in the area (restaurants,stores,and etc). According to pharmacy owner, in the articleThe Barclays Center:Impact on the Community | Brooklyn's Sports:Past,Present,Future , said that the development of the Barclay Center didn't increase profit for them, alot of residents had to leave to make space for the development of Barclays Center, and the development of Barclay Center increased the rent price.
A novel called The Invention of Brownstone Brooklyn. The author called the people that moved from East River of Manhattan to Brooklyn gentrifiers. The landlords would commonly take advantage of gentrification to make changes that best fit people that just started to live in Brooklyn. Most people starting to live in Brooklyn for the first time had white collar jobs. In 1971, a survey was taken by brownstone owners and it revealed that people that just started living in Brooklyn were 99% white, 60% graduated, and 98.7% earning in the top five of New York households. They have alot of buying power that the natives in Brooklyn do not.Therefore, Landlords try to accommodate the people that just started living in Brooklyn than the people living in Brooklyn for years.
More luxurious are being built in Brooklyn. Many of the luxurious buildings in Williamsburg was once a warehouse and factories. For example, there was a factory called Domino Sugar factory. It was in redevelopment before it was shut down and sold to Two Tree Company. Two Tree Company made luxurious apartments which would make the rent more costly. The more luxurious buildings being built will lead to increase of only certain amount of people that can rent the apartments which leads to others moving out causing displacement.
