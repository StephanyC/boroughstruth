Gentrification in Brooklyn

This is a image of Kobe Bryant and his daugther, Vanessa who tragedically died. This mural is an image of their fans morning them. Right above this mural is redevolpment occuring which signifies the gentrification happening in this neighborhoods.
From 2000 to 2010, an increase of 6,700 to 15,6000 white people have moved into neighborhoods that were predonimantly Black and Latino. This lead to the decrease of Black and Latino residents. This is displacement which is when homes or apartments are being left by residents that can not afford the high prices. Brooklyn natives are leaving due to the big increase in price, facing harassments from tenants and witholding repairs for they can get tenats that pay higher. Black Brooklyn is known as areas in Brooklyn that have majority of the African American population. These areas would include Fort Greene, Clintin Hill Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brownsville, Crown Heights, Ocean Hill, Canarsie,Flatlands, East Flatbush, Flatbush, some parts of Bushwick, and some parts of downtown Brooklyn.
During the 1990s to 2000s using data collection from those Census, researchers found that middle class black families gentrifies low class black families because of the neighborhood increased in value and there is racial diversity in the neighborhoods which means experiencing less microagressions and racism. There was a limitation to the study that the census couldn't exactly be precise in identifying gentrification.
The development of the Barclays Center has increased amount of people living in the neighborhood and increased businesses in the area (resturants,shops,and etc). According to pharmacy owner, in the articleThe Barclays Center:Impact on the Community|Brooklyn's Sports:Past,Present,Future , said that the development of the barclay center didn't increase profit for them, alot of residents had to leave to make space for the development of barclays center, and increase the rent price.
